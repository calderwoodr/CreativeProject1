And SNAP -- He's gone.

The coin was in the air. We knew it had to be either Cap or Iron Man (though possibly both). In a heroic moment, Iron Man gave it all up to banish Thanos forever.

How did it happen?

In the finale, Thanos has regained all five infinity stones and prepares to undo all the work the Avengers team had done up until that point. In a light fingered flash, Iron Man purloins the gauntlet and uses it to snap Thanos out of existence...but at the cost of his own life.

They even gave him a family!

Since there haven't been many Iron Man movies as of late, his character growth has been limited to the Avenger movies and Captain America: Civil War. In one final effort to make it tear-jerky, Tony Stark and Pepper Potts finally have a kid.

A plot hole?

Though strongly confirmed in Endgame itself, the canon of snaps being highly dangerous seems like a new thing they needed to add to kill off Iron Man. I mean, in Infinity War, Thanos snapped away half of all life in the universe without even breaking a sweat. Now all of a sudden snapping away only Thanos is life threatening?

Sorry, folks.
